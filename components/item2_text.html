Investment-Grade Economics

For the SPE Asset Management Technical Section's inaugural Energython, I designed a gas-to-data-center investment project from the ground up. I selected a project site near Red Oak, Dallas, to locate the power plant with direct access to the newly constructed Hugh Brinson Pipeline . This strategic positioning allowed for fuel sourcing at a significant discount relative to regional competitors.

Based on the 10-20MW net power delivery request from investors, I conducted a full technical audit of heat rates and mechanical degradation. I ultimately opted for modular engines in an N+2 configuration, ensuring the 99.99% uptime required for high-availability data centers.

After calculating a bottom-up CAPEX and determining an ambitious generation tariff, I modeled the LFCF, DSCR, and IRR for a 15-year horizon, incorporating a MACRS tax shield to optimize project returns.

To finalize the risk assessment, I executed 10,000 Monte Carlo simulations to stress-test the model's sensitivity, presenting the results via a P10-50-90 plot and tornado chart .

Nevada Play Fairway Analysis:
Deep Learning for Geothermal Exploration

For the GEODE Datathon, we were tasked with evaluating the Nevada Play Fairway dataset, an integrated synthesis of geological, geochemical, and geophysical signals used to identify blind geothermal systems.

We took a novel approach by reframing traditional spatial regression as a computer vision problem. After processing and rasterizing spatial features such as geodetic strain and Euclidean distance to faults in ArcGIS, I developed a Pixel-wise CNN Architecture using PyTorch to predict overall site favorability.

By treating the stacked geospatial layers as a multi-channel image tensor, the model successfully captured the non-linear, spatial relationships between faults and heat anomalies. The CNN predictions highly agree with the known training locations, validating its base physical accuracy.

The true value created, however, lies in the model's extrapolation. When evaluating the difference map, our network identified highly prospective new zones (indicated by "darker reds" in the Difference Map) that were undervalued by standard linear methods, yet remain geologically highly promising due to their proximity to local faults and permeability corridors.

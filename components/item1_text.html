PhD Side-Project

Open-access Permian Basin Modeling:
Big Data meets Geology & Petrophysics

While working on my PhD research in the Delaware Basin, I noticed a gap: there is no openly available, basin-wide formation model for the Delaware or the broader Permian. Existing work almost universally relies on proprietary databases like Enverus, creating a barrier for geoscientists who need a reproducible baseline. To address this, I independently began building one from scratch using Texas RRC database and have been working on it to extract the observed formation tops. Initial results demonstrate that we are able to capture the formation tops remarkably from open-access data, as the view significantly matches the existing cross-sections . The next phase of the work is to incorporate the uncertainty coming from the measurement noise and discrepancies, and integration of open-access well-logs, ultimately to produce a benchmark stratigraphic model, followed by ML-driven petrophysical characterization and proxy models for inferring subsurface properties from minimal measurements.
